FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I follow and get updates for this website?

The best way to follow and get updates is to subscribe to RSS Feeds. There are a couple of tools and websites that help you get subscribed, such as Feeder and its Google Chrome extension RSS Feed Reader, or Feedly. I sometimes post links to new blog posts and articles on my Twitter as well, but there is no guarantee for that.

How do I support this website?

If you like this website, sharing the website in your social network, distributing the website to other websites, leaving comments and questions under the website content, and providing constructive feedbacks are the best ways to support. Currently, I don’t seek any financial support for running this website.

What are the major topics this website is covering?

While I don’t limit the topics I will be working on, this website is mainly covering the topics including machine learning, artificial intelligence, quantum computing, mathematics, programming languages, Linux operating system, and physics.

Where do I find code and/or command line instructions related to your projects?

For relatively small code snippets, they are likely available here at stevechanieee.github.io. For larger size code snippets or detailed instructions, they are likely to by found on my GitHub.

Where should I leave questions?

Please leave the questions under the website content as many as you can. This is encouraged because other readers could also see the discussion before they ask duplicated questions. I am currently using Disqus for managing comments and it will routinely notify and remind me about the comments in case I missed or forgot any. If the questions are extremely personal, you could send it to my Email.

Why the comments I left under the articles are not shown?

The comment managing tool Disqus has special spam mechanisms. If your comment is classified as spam, your comment will not show up. I moderate the spam comments on Disqus routinely. If the spam is a false positive, I will make it public manually.

Why some blog posts, articles, and projects can no longer be found on the website?

I wrote some blogs, articles, and projects in my early career during which I don’t understand some of the problems quite well. Looking back at those I feel they are of low-quality and they are not going to be helpful to the readers. Therefore, they are removed.


Starting from 2019, I decided to post things that I know thoroughly and try my best to ensure the quality of the posts. All the mathematics involved, if there is any, should be self-contained and crystal clear to the readers. If you happen to find the links to some invalid posts via search engine or social media and you are still interested in reading those posts, please contact me in person.
